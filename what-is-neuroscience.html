What is Neuroscience?

A simple introduction to the study of the brain and nervous system.

Neuroscience is the scientific study of the brain and the nervous system.

It tries to understand how the brain helps us think, learn, remember, feel emotions, and act in the world. Our brains are dynamic rather than static, therefore our neural activity changes every milisecond. Neural activity is always present, whether you are sleeping or writing an exam; it does depend on what we're engaging with, but the neural activity rarely stops when we do nothing. So even when we think we're doing nothing(like resting), our brain is still awake.

The brain does not work in isolation. It changes as we grow, learn, face challenges, and interact with our environment. That is one reason neuroscience is so interesting: it studies both the structure of the brain and the way experience shapes it. Not only the structure, but how the parts of this structure interact. Because parts alone are static; interaction is what leads to something concrete, whether negative, positive, or neutral.

Our experiences are not only understood by us based on the present, but also based on our past experiences and interactions, making them highly subjective. Two people are likely to experience the same situation differently based on how they grew up.

Neuroscience is a broad field. It includes the study of neurons, memory, sleep, attention, movement, questions about consciousness and identity.

Neuroscience is interesting because it tries to answer something we initially gave to philosophy: the whys' and whats' behind our own identity, beliefs and memory lapse.

Why does it matter?

Understanding neuroscience can help us make sense of learning, behavior, brain health, and the experience of being human.
